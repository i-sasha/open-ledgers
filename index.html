What is Bookkeeping?

It is the process of Systematic Classifying, Recording and Organizing of all Financial Transactions of a Business or a Person. Daily transactions are recorded in Journal(s).
Monthly totals are then Posted to the Ledger. The ledger is basically a Chart of Accounts.
An account is a unique record for each type of asset, liability, equity, revenue and expense.
Based on the information in the ledger, Financial Statements are produced on a periodic basis. Most commonly used statements are Profit and Loss, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow.



Why keep your financial records in an organized and up-to-date state?

For Bookkeeping Records to be useful, they need to be updated on a regular basis, so they reflect current and accurate information. Good record keeping is a legal requirement.
Reliable information is of utmost importance for good business decision making.
Up-to-date records save time and accounting fees, when it comes to taxes.
